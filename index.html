bicep - flex your layout

Lightweight responsive grid layout, written with flexbox.

Standard

Looks and behaves similar to float-based layouts. If you've used a float-based layout framework (like boostrap or foundation) this will feel very familiar.

You choose when your containers break to full width

Resize your browser to see it work. Breakpoints are based on em instead of px so that they work nicely even if a user sets their baseline font size to larger than normal (cmd+ or ctrl+ ). Make your layout work for the user.

Bicep supports reordering

By default, flex items are laid out in DOM order. You can change that with the order attribute.

Use the .item-order-[breakpoint]-[order] classes to rearrange the order that items appear.

Inspect these items to see what orer. You'll also notice that they swap back to the DOM order under the medium breakpoint.

.item-s-4.item-order-m-2
.item-s-5.item-order-m-3
.item-s-3.item-order-m-1

I hear flexbox is good for vertical centering...

You heard right. Apply the .flex-center class to your flex element to center it's child items both vertically and horizontally.
