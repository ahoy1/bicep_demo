bicep - row your layout

Lightweight responsive grid layout, written with flexbox.

Standard

Looks and behaves similar to float-based layouts. If you've used a float-based layout framework (like boostrap or foundation) this will feel very familiar.

.col-md-3
.col-md-6
.col-md-3
Markup 
            
                <div class="row">
                  <div class="col-md-3"></div>
                  <div class="col-md-6"></div>
                  <div class="col-md-3"></div>
                </div>

*Note that you can use .container and .flex independently of one another as needed. Neither depends on the other.

Fluid width

Use .container-fluid for full-width containers.

You choose when your containers break to full width

Resize your browser to see it work. Breakpoints are based on em instead of px so that they work nicely even if a user sets their baseline font size to larger or smaller than normal (cmd+ or ctrl+ ).

Break at small:

.col-xs-3
.col-xs-9

medium:

.col-md-4
.col-md-8

large:

.col-lg-5
.col-lg-7

XL:

.item-xl-4
.item-xl-4
.item-xl-4

or never:

.col-xs-6
.col-xs-6

You can also use multiple classes to define different layout at different sizes:

.col-md-6 .col-lg-3 .item-xl-6
.col-md-6 .col-lg-3 .item-xl-6
.col-md-6 .col-lg-3 .item-xl-3
.col-md-6 .col-lg-3 .item-xl-9
.col-md-6

Bicep supports reordering

By default, row items are laid out in DOM order. You can change that with the order attribute.

Use the .item-order-[breakpoint]-[order] classes to rearrange the order that items appear.

Inspect these items to see what orer. You'll also notice that they swap back to the DOM order under the medium breakpoint.

.col-sm-4.item-order-m-2
.col-sm-5.item-order-m-3
.col-sm-3.item-order-m-1

I hear flexbox is good for vertical centering...

You heard right. Apply the .flex-center class to your flex element to center it's child items both vertically and horizontally.
