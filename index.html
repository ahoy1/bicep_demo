The Grid

Looks and behaves similar to float-based layouts. If you've used a float-based layout framework (like boostrap or foundation) this will feel very familiar.

.col-md-3
.col-md-6
.col-md-3
</> 
                
                    <div class="row">
                      <div class="col-md-3"></div>
                      <div class="col-md-6"></div>
                      <div class="col-md-3"></div>
                    </div>

*Note that you can use .container and .flex independently of one another as needed. Neither depends on the other.

You choose when your containers break to full width

Resize your browser to see it work. Four breakpoints, based on em instead of px so that they work nicely even if a user changes their baseline font size(cmd+/- & ctrl+/- ).

Breakpoint Width Classnames
Extra Large 75em .col-xl-
Large 62em .col-lg-
Medium 48em .col-md-
Small 30em .col-sm-
Extra Small never resizes .col-xs-

Gutters

Give your items a little breathing room. Add the .gutters class to your row container

.col-md-4
.col-md-4
</>

                      <div class="col-md-4"></div>
                      <div class="col-md-4"></div>
                      <div class="col-md-4"></div>
                    </div>

Reordering Columns

By default, row items are laid out in DOM order. You can change that with the order attribute.

Use the .item-order-[breakpoint]-[order] classes to rearrange the order that items appear.

.col-sm-4.col-order-md-2
.col-sm-5.col-order-md-3
.col-sm-3.col-order-md-1
</> 
                
                    <div class="row gutters">
                      <div class="col-sm-4 col-order-md-2"></div>
                      <div class="col-sm-5 col-order-md-3"></div>
                      <div class="col-sm-3 col-order-md-1"></div>
                    </div>

Changing alignment:

flex-align is like text-align for your flex items. Flex items are aligned to "flex-start" (left, usually) by default.

Use the .flex-align-center, .flex-align-end, and .flex-align-start to swap easily between alignments.

Apply the .flex-center class to your .row element to center it's child items both vertically and horizontally.
